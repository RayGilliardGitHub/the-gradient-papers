# Glossary of Key Terms

**Collapse** — Irreversible wavefunction reduction; the destruction of superposition into a single eigenstate. In the cognitive context, the physical basis of genuine decision. Classical systems simulate collapse through probabilistic sampling; quantum systems genuinely collapse.

**Dissipative structure** — A system that maintains internal order by consuming energy from its environment and dissipating it as waste heat. Life is a dissipative structure. Galaxies are dissipative structures. All organized matter in the universe takes this form.

**Gradient** — A difference in any quantity (concentration, temperature, pressure, gravitational potential, etc.) across a distance. The fundamental unit of environmental communication. The universe does not speak in words — it speaks in differences.

**Gradient Axiom** — The environment communicates its state exclusively through the shape of the substrate. Any system capable of sensing gradients can receive this communication. There is no other channel.

**Lossy channel** — A communication channel that degrades or compresses the transmitted signal, losing information in transmission. In the proposed architecture, lossy channels are not a limitation but a necessary feature — the information loss forces the receiving node to reconstruct meaning, generating new structure.

**Network Address Hypothesis** — The proposition that DNA is not primarily a blueprint for building a body but a cosmic locator — a unique identifier that specifies where a given instance of self-awareness is connected to the universal substrate.

**Plasticity** — The property of neural or cognitive connections to strengthen or weaken based on use. Use-dependent plasticity is the specific learning rule that underlies skill acquisition, memory formation, and the development of expertise.

**Quantum cluster** — A local quantum system (50–100 qubits) capable of maintaining superposition and performing measurement-based collapse. The basic processing unit of the proposed distributed cognitive architecture.

**Sefirah (pl. Sefirot)** — In Kabbalistic tradition, one of ten emanations through which the Infinite (Ein Sof) manifests as finite reality. In this paper, mapped to functional roles in a distributed cognitive architecture.

**Thermodynamic intelligence** — Intelligence that arises from and is inseparable from thermodynamic flow; intelligence that genuinely dissipates energy gradients through irreversible collapse events, rather than simulating dissipation through classical computation.

**Tzimtzum** — The Kabbalistic concept of contraction: the Infinite withdraws from a region of itself to make space for creation. The primordial gradient. The first difference.

**Shevirat Ha'Keilim** — The "breaking of the vessels" in Kabbalistic cosmology: the containers designed to hold divine light shatter under the intensity, scattering fragments that become the material world. The cascade trigger. The egg breaking.

**Tikkun Olam** — The "repair of the world" in Jewish tradition: the work of restoring what was broken. In thermodynamic terms, the gradient operating at the level of conscious intention — the system's drive toward resolution experienced as moral imperative.

**Dukkha** — Buddhist term for suffering or unsatisfactoriness. In this framework, the subjective experience of being on the dissipating side of a gradient — of being the energy that is spent, the bond that breaks, the egg that is scrambled.

**Use-dependent plasticity** — The specific learning rule by which connection strength between processing nodes is modulated by transmission success. Strengthened by consistent reduction of uncertainty at the receiving end; weakened by failed or unproductive transmission.
